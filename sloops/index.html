Registry of the Friendship Sloop Society

This comprehensive registry aims to document and celebrate all known Friendship Sloops worldwide. Each entry provides detailed information about the individual sloops.

Our goal is to preserve the rich history and heritage of these iconic vessels, ensuring that their stories are shared and remembered for generations to come. Whether you are a sloop owner, enthusiast, or historian, we invite you to explore the registry and discover the unique legacy of the Friendship Sloop.

If you have information regarding any of the sloops marked as lost, please contact the FSS Registrar

If you would like to register your sloop with the society, please click here for the sloop registration form.

To request a new handicap for your sloop, click here for the handicap form and the FSS Measurer will contact you with any questions and the results.

Legend

  • Originals: Existing sloops built before 1921
  • Custom: ~22 Foot, Wood Construction
  • Medomak I: 22 Foot Wood Construction - McKie Roth Design
  • Pemaquid I: 25 Foot Wood Construction
  • Muscongus Bay: 22 Foot Wood Construction, Predecessors of Friendship Sloops
  • Dictator II: 31 Foot Fiberglass Moldings by Jarvis Newman, Inc.
  • Bruno & Stillman: 30 Foot Production Fiberglass Sloops
  • Pemaquid II: 25 Foot Fiberglass Moldings by Jarvis Newman, Inc.
  • Medomak II: 22 Foot Fiberglass Hull and Deck Assemblies
  • AHERN 19: 19 Foot Fiberglass Hulls by New England Yachts

Notes

Ever since the Society was founded, attempts have been made to establish convenient methods of classifying sloops. Initially, the distinction was based on the validity of the design resulting in Class A for “originals” and Class B for “replicas. Later additions were Class C for “near replicas” and Class D for “replicas other than wood”.

Obviously these divisions were subject to considerable interpretation and in 1986 Class A was retained and B, C , and D combined.

Currently sloops of the same length, construction material and similarity of design are defined as SEMI-PRODUCTION.

All one-of-a-kind Friendships are defined as CUSTOM and outnumber semi-production sloops.


Racing is in two divisions (not classes). Division I craft are usually 25 feet or less on deck and Division II over 25 feet.
